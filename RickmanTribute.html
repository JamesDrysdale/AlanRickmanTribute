Alan Rickman was a British actor and director. He was a member of the Royal Shakespear company and though he was cast n a wide range of roles, including romantic leads and comedic characters, he was perhaps best know for the villains he portrayed. He brought characters such as Hans Gruber (Die Hard), Elliot Marston (Quigley Down Under) and the Sherriff of Nottingham (Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves) to life in a way that made you root for them despite how badly they behaved.

Tribute to an incredible actor

Rickman believed that actors were agents of change, that a film, piece of theatre, music or art could make a difference. He believed it could change the world. He was an agent of change. It's clear from the great many heartwarming and touching tributes from those who knew him best that he left an enduring legacy behind him.

Alan Rickman is undoubtedly one of the greatest actors I will ever work with. He is also, one of the loyalest and most supportive people I’ve ever met in the film industry. He was so encouraging of me both on set and in the years post-Potter. I’m pretty sure he came and saw everything I ever did on stage both in London and New York. He didn’t have to do that. I know other people who’ve been friends with him for much much longer than I have and they all say “if you call Alan, it doesn’t matter where in the world he is or how busy he is with what he’s doing, he’ll get back to you within a day.

People create perceptions of actors based on the parts they played so it might surprise some people to learn that contrary to some of the sterner(or downright scary) characters he played, Alan was extremely kind, generous, self-deprecating and funny. And certain things obviously became even funnier when delivered in his unmistakable double-bass.

As an actor he was one of the first of the adults on Potter to treat me like a peer rather than a child. Working with him at such a formative age was incredibly important and I will carry the lessons he taught me for the rest of my life and career. Film sets and theatre stages are all far poorer for the loss of this great actor and man. - Daniel Radcliffe (from his Google+ account)
Alan was a towering person, phsically, mentally and as an artist. He was utterly distinctive, with a voice that could suggest honey or a hidden stiletto blade, and the profile of a Roman Emperor. He was also a great friend, generous and social. He will be very missed by many. - Helen Mirren
Alan’s enormous strength of character infused every character he played. Who else could have brought such pain and wit to Snape. He used his talent always to make a difference, his production of Rachel Corrie being one of the most powerful examples. I can’t believe he’s gone. - Sigourney Weaver
